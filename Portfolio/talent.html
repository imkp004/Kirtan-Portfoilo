INTRODUCTION

By talking to different types of people at my over past years, I have learned and accomplished good communication skills which helped me better understanding other people. I have gained knowledge in communicating by listining to others, noticing nonverbal cues, and practicing oral communication. It helped me in giving interviews for high level job position and it is very important to have that skill in workplace.

By doing different types of jobs I have learned and understood that what people needs more than knowlegde is an communication which comes in hand for any type of job. Learning how to present, active listining, feedback, respect, confidence, clarity, honesty, and public speaking can help in any trouble situation in any job.

EXAMPLES

  1. One of the best ways to effectively communicate is to listen well. Everyone dislikes speaking with someone who only wants to add their two cents and doesn't take the time to listen to the other person. It will be difficult for you to understand what is being requested of you if you are not a good listener.
  2. Active listening requires paying great attention to what the other person is saying, seeking clarification, and reframing what they say to make sure that they are understood. You can answer more effectively when you actively listen to the other person's words and ideas.
  3. Not talking too much or too little is the key to effective verbal communication. Consider using as few words as you can to communicate your ideas. When chatting to someone in person, on the phone, or via email, be sure to express your needs and desires in a clear and concise manner. If you drone on, your audience will either stop paying attention to you or won't be certain of your exact objectives.
  4. Your coworkers will feel more comfortable speaking openly and honestly with you if you use a pleasant tone, ask a personal question, or simply smile at them. It's crucial to use good manners in all of your communications at work.
  5. Respecting others and their opinions will make people more receptive to your communication. The person will feel valued if you take the time to address them by name, make eye contact, and listen intently while they talk. Keep your attention on the conversation at hand while on the phone.
